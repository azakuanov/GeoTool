Introduction

The following introduction tries to explain all the features of GeoLicious.

Why GeoLicious?

GeoLicious was mainly for one reason: I love to be able to look back years later knowing where I've' been everywhere in my life - but I give this information only reluctantly into the hands of any social networks

As nice as Facebook, Foursquare and Yelp are... the very latest information about my stay, I'd post there only reluctantly. You never know who gets to see it.

I was therefore looking for an app that I can gather this information privately and present it in a nice way - but such thing did not exist - at least not in the form as I had imagined. And so GeoLicious was born!

General

Basically GeoLicious is quite simple. The core of the app is a collection of locations that are linked with check-ins. The whole can be enriched with some information and presented and searched in accordance later. Gradually, the options are to be expanded and extended sense.

Map

The map is the heart of the application. Here I present to you all the places covered with markers for all check-ins.

The following things are possible:

  • Via the Check-in button at the top right can be checked in to the current location. Should this location have been registered already, it is being offered directly. If necessary, a new location can be created.
  • Found on the top left of the map you can search for new places. GeoLicious uses Apple's own search, which is only available since iOS 6.1. At such places found a new check-in can be made about the placed pin.
  • Tap and hold any place on the map to place a new pin that may be used for a checkin.
  • A selected pin on the map can be used via the appearing pop to jump into a filtered list of check-ins.
  • The two icons at the bottom right and top right of the map allow you to quickly move to the current location or the overview of all places.
  • Of course, the card can be arbitrarily enlarged and moved.
  • If your locations are shown grouped (see Settings) tapping such a group zooms directly into it.
  • If you turn your device sideways the map is displayed slightly larger using more space.
  • If desired, the display of the map may be switched to satellite mode. There is an option in the settings.

Check-Ins

In this area you can see the list of all the check-ins - sorted by their date. You can search and filter the list by tapping and edit individual records.

  • Drag the list down a little to show the search and the grouping options.
  • By repeatedly tapping the tab icon for check-ins at the bottom you can jump between the top and bottom of the list back and forth.
  • If you tap on one of the entries, you can get access to a filtered list of all the check-ins to the same place.
  • From such a filtered list out a new check-in can be created via the top right check-in button.
  • By activating the red icon to the right to an entry you will be directed to the details where you can edit it.
  • From a pre-filtered list of entries, another tapping leads also to the details of that entry.
  • By changing the group setting you can control how the entries are grouped together.
  • By the usual wiping gesture you can delete individual entries again.

Locations

The locations are the collection of all places covered by you. Also, this list You can search and filter by tapping and edit individual entries.

  • Drag the list down a little to show the search and the grouping options.
  • By repeatedly tapping the tab icon at the bottom you can jump between to the top and bottom of the list back and forth.
  • If you tap one of the entries, you can get access to a filtered list of all check-ins to this location.
  • From such a filtered list a new check-in can be created via the top right check-in button.
  • By activating the red icon to the right of an entry you will be directed to the details where you can edit it.
  • From a pre-filtered list of entries another tapping leads to the details of that entry.
  • By changing the group setting you can control how entries are grouped together.
  • By the usual wiping gesture you can delete individual entries again.

Settings

In the settings of GeoLicious I tried to make everything important sufficiently controllable. The app doesn't need many options for now.

  • You can disable the automatic check-in globally if you want to maximize your battery life for some time.
  • Notifications for automated checkins and the counter at the app icon may be disabled.
  • Short events of less than 3 minutes may be removed again automatically.
  • By grouping the markers on the map locations are represented by a summarized icon if they are too close together.
  • If you like, you may display the map in satellite mode.
  • The app can create as many backups as you like and restore them when needed. In addition, you can secure any and play back these backups through iTunes file sharing.
  • This introduction, a list of common questions and answers and a listing of recent changes can be found there as well.
  • Via an extra link there you can directly contact me for support. It prepares an email for further processing which is then opened.
  • If necessary, you can set back to zero and discard all entries of the entire database. GeoLicious will then create a backup for you, ready for you to restore.
  • I have a simple demo data integration, which can be activated here as well. Again a backup is created.
